Rating: 5/5AwesomeTV

Pros: This tv is simply amazing for the price especially. Its picture is by far the best I have ever seen. Couple this tv with a Samsung Blue-ray player and you have magic, they instantly recognize each other and link up automatically, even if you are watching cable tv at the time, no changing video streams thru the remote if you don't want to, very convenient. It has true-life technology that enhances any HD signal to make the picture sharper on regular DVD's , HD cable signals, MP4's, MKV's etc. Sports have no LCD lag. Has 4 inputs for HDMI instead of 3 that most other brands were offering in this size LCD with these specs. Tv also has smart technology by sensing light in the room and will brighten or darken subtly based on those factors. Tv has bright picture until you turn off all lights in the bedroom, then it tones down the brightness ever so slightly to adjust to the room and your eyes are not feeling stressed.

Cons: The 120hz seems like it might be un-necessary right now because most of our technology is still in 60 hz, Comcast HD with DVR and most of my blueray movies are still in 60 hz when you pull up the info on the particular file-signal you are watching. My Comcast remotes dont seem to work as well since I hooked up this tv, I have 2 of them and its NOT the batteries, the signal seems to be weaker, you have to point DIRECTLY at the box now, will investigate it more and re-comment if its not the Samsung sucking signal etc.

Other Thoughts: BUY ONE Now you wont regret it!

Rating: 4/5Not perfect

Pros: It's a big-screen TV. Image quality/ghosting/contrast are what I would expect for an LCD set at this price and are really quite good.

Cons: If you don't plan to use this to watch over-the-air broadcasts, you'll be fine. Over-the-air tuner has issues: if the signal is weak or gets weak while watching, it can cause the entire set to become almost unresponsive and the broadcast won't ever return until you turn the set off and on again or switch to some other input source and back to TV. It seems like the weak signal is causing some process running on the set's main processor to consume all the processor's resources indefinitely, making the onscreen options very slow or the picture to resume even when signal strength is restored or the channel is changed. Updated to latest firmware on Samsung's site and it seemed to help a little but it's still a problem.

Other Thoughts: Can't establish an HDCP link over DVI with the PC I'm using, but that might be the PC
